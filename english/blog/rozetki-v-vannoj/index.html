Bathroom flush sockets with tile

Hidden installation in wet zones — when flush porcelain stoneware makes sense and how WAVER STORE does it.

A bathroom with full-height porcelain stoneware is one of the most demanding interiors for details. A standard socket with a plastic frame on top of tile breaks the flat surface and collects grime at the edges. WAVER STORE offers hidden sockets and switches mounted flush with the cladding — you see the same material as the wall. See pricing and catalog or gallery for real examples.

Why flush mounting in a bathroom

  • Aesthetics — monolithic wall without plastic dots on stone or marble texture.
  • Hygiene — fewer protrusions where water and soap collect.
  • Durability — no frame peeling off tile due to humidity cycles.
  • Consistency — matches kitchen backsplash solutions.

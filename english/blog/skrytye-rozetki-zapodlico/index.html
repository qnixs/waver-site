Flush-Mounted Hidden Sockets in Tile: What They Are, Types and Installation

A complete breakdown of flush-mounted hidden sockets — what they are, the types available, where to use them and how much they cost.

A flush-mounted hidden socket is a socket installed level with the surface of tile or porcelain stoneware, without a protruding plastic frame. The result is a seamless wall: the socket blends into the cladding and is barely noticeable. This solution is increasingly chosen for kitchen backsplashes, bathrooms and design interiors where a clean surface geometry matters.

How a hidden socket differs from a regular one

A regular surface-mounted or recessed socket has a plastic frame that protrudes a few millimetres above the tile. A hidden socket is mounted into a special frame beneath the cladding, and its face is covered by a piece of the same tile. The result is a flat surface — no frames, seams or protrusions.

Types of hidden sockets

  • Surface-mounted — the simplest and cheapest option, but the socket clearly protrudes from the wall.
  • Magnetic — the face panel is held by magnets; easier to install, but the surface is not perfectly flat.
  • Bayonet — the panel locks with a twist; more secure than magnetic, but the design is more complex.
  • Flush-mount — the socket is fully recessed into the cladding, with only tile visible outside. The cleanest result — this is the type WAVER STORE produces.

Pros and cons

Pros: a clean look without frames, the socket does not break the tile pattern, it fits minimalist interiors and works for both kitchen and bathroom.

Cons: installation is more complex than a regular socket and requires an accurate tile cut, and the cost is higher than surface-mounted solutions. These trade-offs are offset by the appearance and solved by the precise template included in the kit.

Where hidden sockets are used

  • Kitchen. Flush sockets on the backsplash do not break the tile and stay out of the way of appliances.
  • Bathroom. Hidden installation in porcelain stoneware while respecting safety zones.
  • Living room and bedroom. Flush sockets in a feature wall or TV zone.

How installation works

In short: mark with the template → cut the tile with a Ø42 mm core bit → glue the mounting frame → install the socket → adjust flush → final assembly with the ceramic insert. A full step-by-step guide is available in the PDF manual.
